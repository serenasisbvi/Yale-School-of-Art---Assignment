Graduate Study Areas

Graphic Design (MFA)

Students develop a thesis project over two years, including multiple diverse projects in a cohesive framework. Weekly faculty-led studio sessions, biweekly thesis groups, and lectures, workshops, and presentations are included. Admission: up to 12 students in two-year program.

Painting and Printmaking (MFA)

Integrates painting and printmaking in a critical, experimental environment. Students have individual 300-square-foot studios, access to etching, lithography, silkscreen, and digital resources. Approximately 21 students admitted annually.

Photography (MFA)

A two-year program for ten students per year. Includes darkroom, studio, and digital facilities, technical instruction in black-and-white, color, nonsilver processes, digital image production, and weekly critiques.

Sculpture (MFA)

Students work independently in studios with access to common critique spaces. Resources include woodworking shop, metal shop, and computer lab. Emphasis on constructive critique and historical/artistic context. Admission: approximately 10 students per year.

Non-Degree & Interdisciplinary Areas

Critical Practice, film/video, and interdisciplinary studies are available through elective courses, workshops, and collaborative projects across the University.
